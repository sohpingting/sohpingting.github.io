Design Process of PayMe Web & Mobile App

 Scroll

Problem

PayMe 2012 - 2014

Merchants feedback that the web form for creating the payment collection link for customers is difficult to use even though they liked the idea.

The design goal for PayMe is to allow merchants to collect payment easily and securely on their mobile device instead of filling up a form (see the image of the original online payment form).

PayMe app has been running for 2 years and merchants have feedback that it is simpler and more convenient to use than the online form. Their payment collection volume also increased 5-10%.

You can find information about the app here https://smoovpay.wordpress.com/2013/12/29/smoovpay-has-launched-payme-android-app-to-google-play/

Inspiration Process

Before we got to design version 3, many prototype of version 1 has been done.

Research

  • It started with market research for similar product.
  • Then consolidte feedback from customer support and sales personnel about the current web form.
  • Observing and speak to potential customers who run their business on the move.

Synthesis

  • Mindmap the information gathered to identity connection
  • Output: Identified potential users
  • Output: Identified users scenarios for product brainstorming

Ideation Process

Brainstorming process

Sketch out screens on paper and select a few functions that is useful for the discussion at workshop.

Mini workshop

Usually I would have a draft concept of the product in mind before running a short workshop with stakeholders to validate the concept.

The whole team of product owner, sales, tech, tester and designer would join the brainstorming.

Flow of paper prototype

Implementation Process

HTML Prototype

Due to time constraint, I would jump from paper prototype to interactive prototype.

Test, Iterate, Refine, Repeat

With a clickable prototype I could test the interaction and validate the use of UI.

Did testing with internal users

Technical Check

I would have to create the sitemaps and flow before I start a dicussion with the tech lead and system arthitecture.

After they understand the goal and I understand the technical constraint I would iterate the concept and drafted the functonal design spec with wireframe.

Next, I would go thought the functional design specification document with the main developer and tester.

While developers started on the backend planning and process, I would create the final screens.

Occasionally, I would get the graphic designer to help with the creation of high fidelity screens.

Retrospect

What went well?

Prototyping in the right framework saves lots of time between the paper sketch to actual product.

The design function spec I wrote is very effective in communication with offshore developers.

There are very little miscommunication between design intend and developers.

What could be done better next time?

I should have tested the final prototype personally with targeted business owner.

Clearly define metric to measure success of the product when testing with actual users.
